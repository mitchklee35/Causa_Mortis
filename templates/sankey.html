Sankey Analysis:

From the years 1999 to 2017, the primary causes of death throughout the states was heart disease followed closely by cancer. Heart disease has a major impact on the other systems of the body including the respiratory system. “For example, COPD and fibrosis patients were on average 60% more likely to die after developing heart failure than those with heart failure but no lung disease,” Rahul Potluri, MBChB, founder of the ACALM Study Unit and clinical lecturer at Aston Medical School in Birmingham, England. Respiratory disease of usually not a sole source of death but leads to other complications resulting in the final cause of death being determined as something other than chronic lower respiratory disease (I.e. heart attack).
